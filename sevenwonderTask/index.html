New 7 Wonders of the World

Click a wonder’s name or image to visit its Wikipedia page.

  • Great Wall of China — China

    The Great Wall of China is a vast fortification system built over many centuries. It stretches more than 21,000 kilometers across northern China. Its primary purpose was to protect against invasions from nomadic tribes. Built with stone, brick, and tamped earth, it showcases ancient engineering. Today, it is one of the most visited landmarks in the world.

  • Petra — Jordan

    Petra is an ancient city carved into rose-red sandstone cliffs. It was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom around 300 BC. The most famous site is Al-Khazneh, also called the Treasury. Petra was a key hub for trade routes linking Arabia, Egypt, and Syria. Today, it stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and popular tourist spot.

  • Christ the Redeemer — Brazil

    Christ the Redeemer is a 30-meter tall statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro. It was completed in 1931 and stands on Mount Corcovado. The statue is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone. It is a symbol of Christianity and peace worldwide. Millions of visitors come annually to admire the breathtaking view.

  • Machu Picchu — Peru

    Manchu Picchu is an ancient Incan citadel high in the Andes Mountains. It was built in the 15th century and later abandoned. The site includes temples, terraces, and palaces made of stone. It showcases the Incas’ advanced knowledge of construction. Rediscovered in 1911, it is now a major archaeological wonder.

  • Chichén Itzá — Mexico

    Chichén Itzá was a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people. Its most famous structure is El Castillo, a pyramid dedicated to Kukulcán. The site was a major political and economic center of the Maya civilization. It also served as a place for religious ceremonies and astronomy studies. Today, it is one of Mexico’s most visited archaeological sites.

  • Colosseum — Italy

    The Colosseum in Rome is the largest amphitheater of the ancient world. It was built in AD 70–80 under the emperors Vespasian and Titus. The arena could hold up to 50,000 spectators. It hosted gladiator contests, animal hunts, and public spectacles. Although partly ruined, it remains a symbol of Imperial Rome.

  • Taj Mahal — India

    The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum in Agra, India. It was commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Construction began in 1632 and took about 22 years to complete. The structure combines Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles. It is considered one of the most beautiful symbols of love ever built.
